<Section icon={Swords} title="anime final quest mahito boss Combat Mechanics">

Surviving the **anime final quest mahito boss** encounter requires a deep understanding of his two distinct combat phases. Unlike early-game bosses who rely on basic melee sweeps, this boss utilizes devastating status effects, spikes, and a stage-wide Domain Expansion that can instantly wipe out an unprepared party.

### Phase 1: Subway Skirmish
During the first phase, the boss behaves like a standard high-tier threat. He will attempt to close the distance with rapid basic attacks and occasionally summon spikes from the ground. Floor indicators will briefly flash before the spikes erupt, giving you a short window to dash away. Focus on kiting him around the subway pillars while landing your skills.

### Phase 2: Domain Expansion
Once his health drops below 50%, a dramatic cutscene triggers, signaling his transition into Phase 2. A purple health bar appears at the top of your screen. In this phase, he gains access to his most lethal mechanic: a blinding stun. 

When caught in this attack, your screen goes dark, your skills and dashes are completely disabled, and your character is locked in place. This mobility shutdown makes you highly vulnerable to his high-damage follow-up moves.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Dodge the Initial Burst">
    As soon as the Phase 2 cutscene ends, immediately dash backward to create distance. Do not stay close, as he often opens with a spike summon or a heavy sweep.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Watch for Floor Indicators">
    Keep your eyes on the subway floor. If you see circular purple indicators, dash sideways immediately to avoid the incoming spike attacks.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Bypass the Blindness Stun">
    Use weapons with built-in invincibility frames (i-frames) or specific passive immunities to completely ignore his blinding stun mechanic.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Coordinate Ultimate Skills">
    Wait until he finishes a major attack sequence before unleashing your ultimate skills. This ensures your high-cooldown moves do not get interrupted by his stuns.
  </Step>
</Steps>
