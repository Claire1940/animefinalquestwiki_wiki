<Section icon={Sparkles} title="The Math Behind Traits and Runes: Double Critical Hits">

Many players underestimate the importance of rolling traits with their **anime final quest runes**. However, mathematical testing reveals that traits are the absolute core of your damage scaling [1]. This is due to how the game calculates damage multipliers between your weapon traits and your cosmetic gear [1].

To understand how traits interact with your build, examine these three common setup scenarios:

  <Card title="Scenario C: Superior Trait Meta">
    - **Base Damage**: 900

    - **Result**: Extreme damage output with active double critical hits.
  </Card>

The secret to massive damage lies in the order of operations: the game first applies your weapon's trait to the base damage, and then applies your cosmetic multipliers to that newly buffed value [1]. This means traits act as a multiplicative foundation rather than a simple additive bonus [1]. 

Furthermore, traits enable a unique mechanic known as the **Double Critical Hit** [1]. The critical hit chance on your weapon trait is calculated separately from the critical hit chance on your cosmetics [1]. If both checks succeed simultaneously, you land a double critical hit, multiplying your damage exponentially [1].

| Trait Name | Primary Stat Boost | Tier | Build Synergy |
| Superior | Skill Damage & Critical Chance | S | Enables double critical hits; highest peak damage |
| Accuracy 3 | 15% Skill Damage | A | Highly consistent; perfect for sustained DPS |
| Critical Chance | Higher Critical Rate | B | Decent for double crits, but lacks raw skill scaling |
| Unleashed | Raw Power Multiplier | B | Strong flat damage, but cannot trigger double crits |

For example, using Sasuke's weapon with max skill damage cosmetics (330% skill damage and 25% crit chance), a standard non-crit hit does 4,428 damage [1]. A normal critical hit deals 7,380 damage [1]. However, when a double critical hit triggers, the damage sky-rockets to 14,760 [1]. This massive jump is why rolling for the Superior trait using your runes is highly recommended for late-game builds [1].

<Callout type="tip" title="Accuracy 3 vs. Superior">
While the Superior trait offers the highest peak damage due to double critical hits, Accuracy 3 remains an incredibly strong and highly consistent alternative [1]. If you are low on runes, keeping Accuracy 3 is a smart choice until you can hoard enough resources to safely roll for Superior [1].
</Callout>
