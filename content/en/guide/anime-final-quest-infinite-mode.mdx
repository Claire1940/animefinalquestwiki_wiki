<Section icon={Target} title="In-Wave Upgrade Strategy">

Once you are inside the run, you will be presented with random upgrades every few waves. Making the wrong choice here can end a run prematurely, even with the best gear. The scaling of enemies is aggressive; by Wave 50, standard M1 (basic attacks) become almost entirely useless for most weapons.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Prioritize Skill Boost">
    Always select **Skill Boost** whenever it appears. This is a multiplicative damage increase that ensures your abilities can one-shot mobs as their health pools grow exponentially.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Manage Skill Cooldown">
    Once your damage is sufficient to clear waves comfortably, focus on **Skill Cooldown**. The goal is to reach a state where you can chain abilities back-to-back, utilizing their built-in i-frames to remain untouchable.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Utility & Survival">
    If damage and cooldown options are unavailable, pick **Speed** or **Health**. Speed is particularly useful for "training" mobs (grouping them up) before unleashing a high-AoE attack.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Avoid M1 Boosts">
    Never pick M1 Damage in Infinite Mode. Even with specialized weapons like Chainsaw Man, the scaling of basic attacks cannot keep up with enemy armor and HP in late-game waves.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Upgrade Priority | Value | Reason |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Skill Boost** | **S-Tier** | Essential for keeping up with enemy HP scaling |
| **Skill Cooldown** | **A-Tier** | Allows for infinite i-frame chaining |
| **Movement Speed** | **B-Tier** | Helps in dodging ranged Wizard attacks |
| **Health** | **C-Tier** | Only useful for surviving accidental hits |
| **M1 Damage** | **F-Tier** | Completely useless after Wave 40 |

<Callout type="success" title="Strategy Success">
By focusing exclusively on Skill Boost and Cooldown, even a beginner player can reach Wave 80-100. This provides a solid foundation of 10-15 Golem Hearts per run, translating to 100k-150k gold.

<Section icon={Shield} title="Combat Tactics and Enemy Management">

The battlefield in Infinite Mode is chaotic. Success requires more than just high damage; you must understand enemy priority and how to manage the "Blind" modifier. Ranged enemies, specifically Wizards and Orc Archers, are the primary run-killers because they can chip away at your health from off-screen.

**Priority Target List:**
1.  **Wizards/Ranged Orcs**: Take these out immediately. Their projectiles can stun or interrupt your skill chains.
2.  **Bosses**: While they have high HP, their attacks are telegraphed. Use them to charge your ultimate.
3.  **Zombies/Trash Mobs**: Group these together. Do not waste individual skills on single mobs; "train" them into a tight pack first.

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Reward Scaling and Efficiency Benchmarks">

In Anime Final Quest Infinite Mode, rewards do not scale linearly. There is an exponential growth phase followed by a linear phase. To maximize your time, you need to know when to "End Waves" and restart rather than pushing for world records.

| Wave Milestone | Rune Drop Rate | Golem Heart Yield | Efficiency Rating |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Wave 50** | 1 per 10 waves | 5-8 | Low |
| **Wave 100** | 1 per 8 waves | 12-18 | Medium |
| **Wave 150** | 1 per 5 waves | 25-35 | **Optimal (Sweet Spot)** |
| **Wave 300+** | 1 per 7 waves | 40+ | Diminishing Returns |

Data suggests that **Wave 150** is the "Sweet Spot." Beyond this point, the time it takes to clear a single wave increases significantly, but the reward frequency (Runes and Ores) begins to stabilize or even slow down relative to time spent.

<Callout type="tip" title="Efficiency Secret">
It is often more profitable to do two runs to Wave 150 than one marathon run to Wave 300. You will earn more Runes and Ores per hour by resetting once the enemy HP becomes so high that your skill rotation no longer one-shots the mobs.
</Callout>
